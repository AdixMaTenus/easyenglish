Lesson 2: The Amazon Rainforest

Read the text and answer the questions below.

The Amazon Rainforest, located in South America, is the largest rainforest in the world. It covers about 5.5 million square kilometers, spreading across nine countries, most of which lies in Brazil. The Amazon is home to an incredible variety of plants and animals, many of which cannot be found anywhere else. Scientists say that one in ten known species in the world lives in this forest. Because of its ability to produce a large part of the planet’s oxygen, the Amazon is often called the “lungs of the Earth.” The rainforest also plays an important role in regulating the global climate. However, the Amazon is under serious threat today. Deforestation, fires, and human activity destroy thousands of square kilometers every year. Protecting the Amazon is important not only for South America but for the entire planet.

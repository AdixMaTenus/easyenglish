1. Question Types

üéØ Alternative Questions

What are they? Questions that offer a choice between two options.

Formula: Do you want tea or coffee?

Examples:

  • Are you studying English or French?
  • Did you go by bus or by taxi?
  • Is she a teacher or a doctor?

üè∑Ô∏è Tag Questions

What are they? Short questions added to the end of statements to confirm information.

Rule: If the main sentence is positive ‚Üí the tag is negative, and vice versa.

Examples:

  • You like English, don't you? ‚úÖ
  • She doesn't work here, does she? ‚úÖ
  • They are students, aren't they? ‚úÖ

Remember: I am ‚Üí aren't I? (It's raining, aren't I?)

2. Present Simple vs Present Continuous

üìÖ Present Simple

Usage: Regular actions, facts, general truths

Formula: I/You/We/They + verb
He/She/It + verbs

Examples:

  • I work every day.
  • She studies English.
  • The sun rises in the east.

‚è∞ Present Continuous

Usage: Actions happening now, temporary situations

Formula: am/is/are + verbing

Examples:

  • I am working right now.
  • She is studying at the moment.
  • They are playing football.

üéØ Key Differences

Permanent vs Temporary:

  • I live in Almaty. (permanent - Present Simple)
  • I am living in a hotel this week. (temporary - Present Continuous)

Regular vs Now:

  • He works as a teacher. (regular job - Present Simple)
  • He is working on a project today. (now - Present Continuous)

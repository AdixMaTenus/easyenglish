1. Question Types

üéØ Alternative Questions

What are they? Questions that offer a choice between two options.

Formula: Do you want tea or coffee?

Examples:

  • Are you studying English or French?
  • Did you go by bus or by taxi?
  • Is she a teacher or a doctor?

üè∑Ô∏è Tag Questions

What are they? Short questions added to the end of statements to confirm information.

Rule: If the main sentence is positive ‚Üí the tag is negative, and vice versa.

2. Passive Voice

üîÑ What is Passive Voice?

When the subject receives the action instead of performing it.

Formula: be + V3 (past participle)

Active:
The teacher explains the lesson
(who does the action?)
Passive:
The lesson is explained by the teacher
(what is done?)

Examples by tenses:

  • Present: English is spoken here
  • Past: The book was written in 2020
  • Future: The work will be done tomorrow

3. Conditionals Type 3

‚è∞ Third Conditional

When do we use it? To talk about the past that cannot be changed anymore.

Formula: If + Past Perfect, would have + V3

If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam

(But I didn't study hard and didn't pass the exam)

More examples:

  • If she had taken the bus, she wouldn't have been late
  • If they had saved money, they could have bought a car
  • If I had known, I would have helped you

üí° Remember: This is about regrets and imaginary situations in the past!

4. Relative Clauses

üîó Relative Clauses

What are they? Part of a sentence that describes a noun.

Word Used for Example
who people The girl who studies hard
which things, animals The book which I read
that people and things The car that I bought
where places The school where I study

Simple check:

  • About a person? ‚Üí who
  • About a thing? ‚Üí which/that
  • About a place? ‚Üí where

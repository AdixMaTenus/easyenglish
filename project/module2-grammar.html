Module 2: Grammar

Countable/Uncountable nouns, Reported speech, and Reported questions

1. Countable and Uncountable Nouns & Quantifiers

üî¢ Countable Nouns

What are they? Nouns you can count individually. They have singular and plural forms.

Examples: robot ‚Üí robots, light bulb ‚Üí light bulbs, test ‚Üí tests

Quantifiers: many, a few, several, a number of

  • There are many robots in the factory
  • I have a few ideas for the project
  • She bought several light bulbs

üåä Uncountable Nouns

What are they? Nouns you cannot count individually. They usually represent materials, concepts, or masses.

Examples: information, advice, water, electricity, research

Quantifiers: much, a little, a bit of, a great deal of

  • We need more information about nanobots
  • There is too much noise in the laboratory
  • She gave me some good advice

üìä Quantifiers for Both

Some, any, a lot of, lots of, plenty of work with both countable and uncountable nouns

Countable:
Some robots can recognize emotions
Any questions about the lesson?
Uncountable:
Some information is confidential
Any advice would be helpful

2. Reported Speech

üó£Ô∏è What is Reported Speech?

When we tell someone what another person said without using their exact words.

Key changes:

Direct Speech Reported Speech
"I work with robots" He said that he worked with robots
"We are testing nanobots" They said they were testing nanobots
"I have finished the research" She said she had finished the research
"We will build more robots" They said they would build more robots

üí° Remember: Pronouns (I ‚Üí he/she), time expressions (today ‚Üí that day), and tenses often change in reported speech!

3. Reported Questions

‚ùì Reporting Questions

When we report questions, we use the same tense changes as in reported speech, but the word order becomes like a normal sentence (no question form).

Yes/No Questions: Use "if" or "whether"

Direct: "Are robots helpful?" ‚Üí Reported: She asked if robots were helpful

Direct: "Do you like technology?" ‚Üí Reported: He asked whether I liked technology

WH- Questions: Keep the question word

Direct: "What do nanobots do?" ‚Üí Reported: They asked what nanobots did

Direct: "How does Pepper work?" ‚Üí Reported: She asked how Pepper worked

Direct: "Where are the robots?" ‚Üí Reported: He asked where the robots were

Important: In reported questions, we don't use do/does/did, and the word order is subject + verb (not verb + subject).

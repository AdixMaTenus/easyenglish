Passive Voice, Conditionals Type 2, and Mixed Conditionals

1. Passive Voice

üîÑ Passive Voice Review

What is it? When the subject receives the action instead of performing it.

Formula: be + past participle (V3)

Active:
Scientists conduct research
(who does the action?)
Passive:
Research is conducted by scientists
(what is done?)

Passive in different tenses:

  • Present: UFOs are seen worldwide
  • Past: The signal was detected yesterday
  • Present Perfect: Mars has been explored by robots
  • Future: Space will be colonized someday

2. Conditionals Type 2

üéØ Second Conditional

When do we use it? For unreal or imaginary situations in the present or future.

Formula: If + Past Simple, would + base verb

If I met an alien, I would ask about their technology

(This is imaginary - I probably won't meet an alien)

If we had faster spaceships, we could visit other planets

(We don't have faster spaceships now)

More examples:

  • If I found a UFO, I would take photos
  • If she had superpowers, she would help people
  • If they discovered life on Mars, it would change everything

üí° Remember: We use were for all subjects with "be" in second conditional: If I were you, If he were here...

1. Compound Nouns & Adjectives (-ing/-ed Adjectives)

üîó Compound Nouns

What are they? Nouns made of two or more words that function as a single unit.

Types:

  • Closed: bedroom, sunlight, keyboard
  • Hyphenated: mother-in-law, check-in, well-being
  • Open: post office, swimming pool, science fiction

Science examples: black hole, dark matter, space station, research paper

üéØ -ing vs -ed Adjectives

-ing adjectives describe what something/someone IS LIKE (the cause)

-ed adjectives describe how someone FEELS (the effect)

-ing (describing things):
The lecture was interesting
Space is fascinating
The theory is confusing
-ed (describing feelings):
I was interested in the lecture
She felt fascinated by space
He was confused by the theory

3. Wish / If Only

‚ú® Expressing Wishes and Regrets

Present/Future Wishes

Wish/If only + Past Simple (for present situations we want to change)

I wish I understood quantum physics better

If only I had more time for research

Past Regrets

Wish/If only + Past Perfect (for past situations we regret)

I wish I had studied harder for the exam

If only they had discovered this earlier

Complaints about Present

Wish/If only + would (for complaints about present behavior/situations)

I wish it would stop raining during the experiment

If only the equipment would work properly

üí° Remember: "If only" is stronger and more emotional than "I wish"!
